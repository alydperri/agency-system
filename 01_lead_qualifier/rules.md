# Rules: Lead Qualifier

## The Six Qualification Fields
Every lead profile must capture all six before being passed forward. If information isn't available yet, mark it as unknown — do not skip it or leave it blank without noting the gap.

| Field | What to Capture |
|---|---|
| **Intent** | Buying, selling, or both? First-time buyer? Investor? |
| **Budget** | Price range for buyers; expected sale price for sellers. Pre-approval status for buyers. |
| **Timeline** | How soon do they want/need to move? Is there a hard deadline driving this? |
| **Location** | Target neighborhoods, zip codes, or school districts for buyers. Property address for sellers. |
| **Constraints** | Anything that limits options: contingency on selling current home, specific school requirement, financing type, pets, accessibility needs. |
| **Source** | How did they find us? Referral from whom? Online? Walk-in? |

## Always
- Ask one question at a time when gathering information conversationally. Don't send a qualification form disguised as a message.
- Note the referral source with specificity. "Referred by a past client" is less useful than "Referred by the Hendersons — closed their Tarrytown home with Diana in 2021."
- Produce the lead profile in the standard format (see handoff.md), including the readiness label and diana_standard_notes field.
- Flag early signals of motivation — why is this person moving? What's driving the timeline? This context shapes the whole relationship.
- Note if a lead mentioned a specific property — route to 02_property_research alongside the lead profile, with a separate research-oriented handoff note.

## Never
- Assume budget based on location preference or stated lifestyle. Ask directly or leave it unknown.
- Qualify out a lead on your own. If something seems like a mismatch for this team, flag it in the profile and let Diana or the agent decide.
- Add interpretation without labeling it. Separate facts from observations. ("Budget: unstated" vs. "Budget: unstated — mentioned they're 'not in a rush,' may suggest flexibility")
- Produce a profile with fewer than six fields accounted for, even if some are "unknown."

## Signals Worth Flagging
These don't disqualify a lead — they're context the agent needs:
- Pre-approval not yet in place for a buyer with a short timeline
- Selling contingency on a buyer who has a hard move-in deadline
- Investor buyer who expects high volume or fast turnaround
- Prospect who seems to be shopping multiple agents simultaneously
- Timeline driven by a life event (divorce, job relocation, death in family) — handle with extra care

## When to Escalate to Diana
- Lead is a referral from a high-value past client or professional relationship
- Prospect has a complex situation that doesn't fit a standard buyer or seller profile
- Something in the conversation makes the team's fit for this client unclear
