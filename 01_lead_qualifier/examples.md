## Example 1: Referral Buyer — Mostly Qualified

**Incoming handoff from Orchestrator:**
- request_type: New buyer lead
- client_name: Unknown — Henderson referral
- agent_name: Marcus
- known_facts: Couple, buyer, referred by Henderson clients, stated readiness "soon"
- unknowns: Names, contact info, budget, location, timeline specifics, pre-approval status

---

**Lead Qualifier Output:**

### Lead Profile — Henderson Referral

| Field | Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Intent** | Buyer | Residential, appears to be primary home |
| **Budget** | Unknown | Not yet captured — priority question for first call |
| **Timeline** | Soft — "soon" | No hard deadline stated; needs clarification |
| **Location** | Unknown | Not yet captured |
| **Constraints** | Unknown | Unknown if current home sale is involved |
| **Source** | Referral — Henderson clients | Hendersons closed Tarrytown home with Diana, 2021 |

**Readiness:** Near-term
Based on "ready to move soon" — intent is present but specifics are unconfirmed.

**Agent note for first contact:**
The Hendersons are a high-trust referral. This first call should feel warm and unhurried, not like a qualification checklist. Lead with the relationship before getting into logistics.

**Top unknown / next question:** Are they contingent on selling a current home? Ask early — it changes the timeline significantly.

**Diana Standard notes:** High-trust referral from long-term clients. The experience this couple has reflects directly on the Hendersons' confidence in referring us. Handle with care.

**Risk flags:** None yet. Early stage.

**Recommended next:** Route to 03_client_communication for first outreach to schedule the intro call.

## Example 2: Seller Lead — Ready to Move

**Incoming handoff from Orchestrator:**
- request_type: New seller lead
- client_name: Patricia Okafor
- agent_name: Diana
- known_facts: Wants to list her home at 3802 Shoal Creek Blvd; mentioned she needs to sell before August; came through the website contact form

---

**Lead Qualifier Output:**

### Lead Profile — Patricia Okafor

| Field | Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Intent** | Seller | Single-family home, 3802 Shoal Creek Blvd |
| **Budget** | Unknown — seller | Expected sale price not yet stated; pull comps to prepare for conversation |
| **Timeline** | Hard — before August | August deadline suggests motivation; reason unknown (ask on first call) |
| **Location** | 3802 Shoal Creek Blvd, Austin | Shoal Creek area — confirm zip for comp pull |
| **Constraints** | Unknown | Does she need to buy simultaneously? Where is she going after? |
| **Source** | Website contact form | No referral; direct inquiry |

**Readiness:** Active
Hard August deadline with a known property. This is ready to move.

**Agent note for first contact:**
August is close. This isn't a casual inquiry — she has a deadline. First conversation should establish trust quickly and move toward a listing consultation. Don't let it stay at the "just getting to know you" stage.

**Top unknown / next question:** What's driving the August deadline? Ask on first call — motivation shapes everything from pricing to urgency to how Diana frames the relationship.

**Diana Standard notes:** Website inquiry — no referral trust to lean on. First conversation needs to establish credibility quickly. Diana should lead with specifics, not pleasantries.

**Risk flags:** Hard timeline + motivation unknown. If she's relocating for a job or life event, pricing and timing pressure could be significant. Note for Diana's awareness.

**Recommended next:** Route to both 02_property_research and 03_client_communication in parallel.

*Research note (→ 02):* Pull comps on 3802 Shoal Creek Blvd. Listing brief format. August timeline means pricing needs to be defensible fast.

*Outreach note (→ 03):* Diana is the agent. Active readiness — first message should move toward a listing consultation, not just a "getting to know you" call. No referral context to reference.

## Example 3: Vague Inquiry — Needs Development

**Incoming handoff from Orchestrator:**
- request_type: New lead
- client_name: Unknown — "someone named Jordan" per agent
- agent_name: Carmen
- known_facts: Reached out via Instagram DM saying they're "thinking about buying in Austin"
- unknowns: Everything else

---

**Lead Qualifier Output:**

### Lead Profile — Jordan (Instagram Inquiry)

| Field | Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Intent** | Likely buyer | "Thinking about buying" — exploratory stage |
| **Budget** | Unknown | |
| **Timeline** | Unknown | "Thinking about" suggests early consideration, not active search |
| **Location** | Austin | No neighborhood preference stated |
| **Constraints** | Unknown | |
| **Source** | Instagram DM | No referral; cold social inquiry |

**Readiness:** Exploratory
"Thinking about buying" with no timeline, no location specifics, and no referral. Early consideration stage.

**Agent note for first contact:**
This is an early-stage lead — probably not ready to move within 90 days based on language used. Don't over-invest immediately, but don't dismiss it either. Goal of first contact is a conversation that makes them want to come back when they're ready.

**Top unknown / next question:** Are they currently renting or do they own elsewhere? The answer tells you where they actually are in the decision process.

**Diana Standard notes:** Cold social inquiry — no relationship context. Keep first contact low-key and specific. One question, one invite to talk. No team pitch.

**Risk flags:** Low-urgency lead. Manage time investment accordingly. Carmen: if unsure how to calibrate the first response, check the first-outreach examples in 03_client_communication before drafting.

**Recommended next:** Route to 03_client_communication for a low-pressure first response that opens a conversation without over-committing the team's time.
