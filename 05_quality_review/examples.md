## Example 2: Draft Returned for Revision — Cliché and Vague

**Incoming draft from 03_client_communication:**

*Draft — first outreach email to new buyer lead, agent: Priya*

> Hi Sarah and David,
>
> I'm so excited to connect with you! Finding your dream home is such an exciting journey and I'd love to be part of it. Austin is a competitive market right now and great homes move fast, so it's important to get started as soon as possible. I specialize in helping buyers find the perfect home and I know I can help you too.
>
> Let's set up a time to chat and get the ball rolling! Looking forward to connecting soon.
>
> Priya

---

**Quality Review:**

### Quality Review — First Outreach to Sarah and David

**Verdict:** Revise with notes

**What's working:**
Clear call to action (schedule a call). Appropriate length for a first email.

**Notes for revision:**
- "So excited," "dream home," "exciting journey," "competitive market," "great homes move fast," "get the ball rolling," "looking forward to connecting" — all of these are clichés. Remove all of them. Every single one.
- The email is entirely about Priya. There is one mention of Sarah and David and it's generic. Reframe: who are they and what are they looking for? That's what the first email should be about.
- "Great homes move fast so it's important to get started as soon as possible" — this is pressure language that this team doesn't use on first contact. Remove.
- "I know I can help you too" — overclaims. Priya doesn't know that yet. Remove.
- Lead with something that makes Sarah and David feel seen, not with Priya's enthusiasm.

**Flag for Diana:** No — but Priya may benefit from reviewing the client communication examples before the next draft.

### Quality Review — Patricia Okafor Appraisal Gap Email
