# Examples: Client Communication

---

## Example 1: First Outreach — Referral Lead

**Incoming handoff from Lead Qualifier:**
- client_name: [Unknown — Henderson referral, couple, buyers]
- agent_name: Marcus
- context: Warm referral from past clients. First contact. Goal is to schedule a call.
- voice notes: Marcus — conversational, not formal. The Hendersons are good clients, treat this like a warm intro.

---

**Draft (text message):**

Hi — this is Marcus with Diana's team. The Hendersons mentioned you're thinking about buying in Austin and suggested I reach out. Congrats on the next chapter.

I'd love to connect briefly — even just 20 minutes — to hear what you're looking for and see if we'd be a good fit to help. No agenda, just a conversation.

When's a good time this week?

— Marcus

---

*Agent note: Kept it short and low-pressure. No team pitch. The Hendersons are the trust signal — letting that do the work.*

## Example 2: Competing Offer — Buyer in Active Situation

**Incoming handoff from Orchestrator:**
- client_name: The Nguyens
- agent_name: Marcus
- situation: Competing offer just came in on 5514 Duval St. Nguyens have until 5pm tomorrow to respond. They need to decide whether to hold, escalate, or walk.
- voice notes: They're cautious buyers. Don't amp up the urgency — they don't respond well to pressure. Be clear, not dramatic.

---

**Draft (email):**

Subject: Update on 5514 Duval — another offer came in

Hi [first names],

Quick update: the sellers received another offer today. They've asked all interested buyers to submit their best offer by 5pm tomorrow.

Here's where that leaves you:

You have until tomorrow at 5pm to decide whether to hold your current offer, strengthen it, or step back. There's no obligation to escalate — if this property is right but the price ceiling isn't, that's a completely valid call.

I'd suggest we talk today if you can. It doesn't need to be long — 15 minutes to walk through your options and make a decision you're comfortable with.

What time works for you this afternoon or evening?

Marcus

---

*Agent note: Kept urgency factual, not emotional. Message frames the decision clearly without pressuring them to escalate. Call offer is the right move — this situation deserves a real conversation.*

## Example 3: Inspection Issue — Seller Response

**Incoming handoff from Transaction Coordinator:**
- client_name: Patricia Okafor (seller)
- agent_name: Diana
- situation: Buyer's inspection flagged an HVAC unit that's 18 years old and functioning but near end of life. Buyer is requesting a $4,200 credit. Patricia is frustrated and wants to push back.
- voice notes: Diana — direct and calm. Patricia trusts Diana's read. Don't sugarcoat, but don't catastrophize either.

---

**Draft (text to Patricia, then suggest a call):**

Hi Patricia — Diana here. Got the inspection report back. The main ask from the buyer is a $4,200 credit for the HVAC unit. It's functioning, but it's 18 years old and they want some cushion.

Here's my honest read: this is a reasonable ask given the age of the unit. We can negotiate — I'd counter at $2,500 — but fighting it entirely carries some risk of reopening other conversations.

I want to walk you through the options before we respond. Are you free for a quick call today?

---

*Agent note: Patricia's frustration is valid, but the ask isn't unreasonable. Diana's voice here is "I'm on your side and I'm also going to tell you what's true." Draft reflects that.*

## Example 4: Transaction Check-In — Keeping Buyer Informed

**Incoming handoff from Transaction Coordinator:**
- client_name: The Nguyens
- agent_name: Marcus
- situation: Two weeks into contract. No issues. Buyers haven't heard anything in a few days and may be anxious.
- purpose: Proactive check-in to confirm things are on track

---

**Draft (text):**

Hi — checking in on the Duval Street timeline. Everything is moving as expected: inspection is done, title work is in process, and we're tracking toward your closing date. Nothing you need to do right now.

I'll reach out as soon as anything needs your attention. You're in good shape.

— Marcus

---

*Agent note: Short. No fluff. Reassurance through specificity, not through "everything is great!" language.*
